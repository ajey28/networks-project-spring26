# Analysis

## Highest Inefficiency Ratio — London

The city with the highest inefficiency ratio is London. The cables that serve it include Apollo, AEConnect, and EXA North and South. This explains the result as other factors such as queuing delays, routing hops, and TCP handshakes causes the overhead to be high to the point where measured RTT is smaller than the theoretical minimum.

## Closest to the Theoretical Minimum — Mumbai

The city closest to the theoretical minimum would be Mumbai. This tells us that the routing infrastructure in Mumbai is extremely well optimized, mainly running in straight lines, Google has lots of infrastructure in the area, and the quality must be among the very best around the world.

## Why Africa Routes Through Europe

Africa routes through Europe because many submarine cables connect Lagos to Europe and because there has traditionally been less internet exchange points in Africa. This means that a lack of IXPs result in local traffic to be much slower, so it is compensated by taking expensive detours through Europe.

## What Would Need to Change

Some things that would need to change in order to fix it includes:

- **Direct transatlantic cable** — Having a direct cable from the east coast of America to west Africa would prevent the current expensive detours being taken to Europe at this time.
- **More African IXPs** — This can be done by keeping traffic local at a high percentage, mainly in the seventies or eighties.
- **Local content hosting** — Hosting content locally so that African users use European data centers less often.
